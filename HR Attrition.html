HUMAN RESOURCES: ANALYSING EMPLOYEE ATTRITION

Empowering you to retain and enable your people for optimal growth

December 15, 2023 IreOluwa Ige
Click to view/download the project file

Background

Employees leave their organizations for unpredictable or uncontrollable reasons. The most common reasons include termination, resignation, long-term illness, voluntary or planned retirement, layoffs, structural changes and relocation. The good news is that your company structure is overall the best enabler of your business growth. Ultimately, without the people who build, maintain and sell your product, your business does not exist. So organising, retaining and enabling your people for optimal growth is the best thing you can do to achieve growth.

Scope

This Power BI project makes use of 1 raw CSV file containing HR employee data such as age band, employee number, department, gender, job role, marital status, office distance from home, etc. The goal is to create a HR report/dashboard that leverages DAX functions, filters, measures and Power Query to clean the data for analysis in order to extract insights that will reduce the attrition rate to 10% or less. The results will help to drive better decision-making regarding talent management, employee engagement, and organizational performance.

Conclusion

The analysis shows that those employed into the sales and R&D department have really high churn rate. It also shows that employees with 0-10 years of working with the company exit at 3.5 times those that have spent 11-25 years at the company. That is, exits reduce the longer the employee stays. Finally, staff with Life Science and Medical backgrounds record the highest church rates. This could be due to the high demand and competitive remuneration offered by other companies in the industry. Thus the compny needs to design both financial and social benefits that will retain these employees.
